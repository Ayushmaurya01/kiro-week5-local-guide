# Prayagraj Local Street Food Context

## City Overview
Prayagraj (formerly Allahabad) is the cultural heart of Uttar Pradesh, famous for its confluence of rivers (Sangam) and incredible street food culture. The city comes alive during Kumbh Mela and has a unique food ecosystem.

## Local Slang & Phrases
- **Bhai** - Brother (friendly address)
- **Teekha** - Spicy
- **Halka** - Light/mild
- **Nashta** - Breakfast/snacks
- **Shaam** - Evening
- **Subah** - Morning
- **Kumbh area** - Area around the famous Kumbh Mela grounds
- **Sangam** - Confluence of rivers, holy area
- **Chowk** - Market area/square
- **Gali** - Lane/street
- **Meetha** - Sweet
- **Bhookh lagi hai** - Feeling hungry
- **Kya milega** - What's available?

## Street Food Categories

### Morning (Subah) - 6 AM to 11 AM
**Kachori Sabzi**
- Description: Crispy deep-fried bread with spicy potato curry
- Local Style: Best at Civil Lines area, served piping hot with green chutney
- Local Tip: Ask for "extra sabzi" for more curry

**Jalebi**
- Description: Sweet spiral-shaped dessert, crispy and syrupy
- Local Style: Fresh and warm, orange-colored, best with milk
- Local Tip: Morning jalebis are crispier than evening ones

**Samosa Chaat**
- Description: Broken samosas topped with chutneys, yogurt, and spices
- Local Style: Crushed samosa with tangy tamarind water
- Local Tip: Ask for "meetha chutney zyada" for extra sweet sauce

### Afternoon (Dopahar) - 12 PM to 4 PM
**Pani Puri (Gol Gappa)**
- Description: Crispy hollow puris filled with spiced water
- Local Style: Served with mint water and tamarind water
- Local Tip: Say "teekha paani" for spicy water, "meetha paani" for sweet

**Aloo Tikki Chaat**
- Description: Fried potato patties with chutneys and toppings
- Local Style: Served on banana leaf with yogurt and sev
- Local Tip: Best at Chowk area, ask for "dahi zyada"

**Dahi Bhalla**
- Description: Soft lentil dumplings in yogurt with chutneys
- Local Style: Cold and refreshing, perfect for hot afternoons
- Local Tip: Popular near Sangam area for pilgrims

### Evening (Shaam) - 5 PM to 9 PM
**Chhole Bhature**
- Description: Spicy chickpea curry with fried bread
- Local Style: Served with pickled onions and green chilies
- Local Tip: Evening portions are larger, perfect for sharing

**Kulfi Faluda**
- Description: Dense ice cream with vermicelli and rose syrup
- Local Style: Served in earthen pots, flavored with cardamom
- Local Tip: Best after spicy food, try pistachio flavor

**Kebabs**
- Description: Grilled meat skewers with mint chutney
- Local Style: Cooked on coal, served with roomali roti
- Local Tip: Evening kebabs are juicier, try seekh kebab

### Night (Raat) - 9 PM onwards
**Bedmi Puri with Aloo Sabzi**
- Description: Spiced lentil-stuffed puris with potato curry
- Local Style: Heavy meal, perfect for dinner
- Local Tip: Popular near railway station area

**Rabri**
- Description: Thick sweetened milk dessert
- Local Style: Garnished with nuts and silver leaf
- Local Tip: Best way to end a heavy meal

## Area-Specific Recommendations

### Kumbh Area
- Famous for: Simple, pure vegetarian food
- Best for: Pilgrims and tourists
- Specialties: Puri sabzi, lassi, simple sweets
- Timing: Early morning to evening

### Civil Lines
- Famous for: Upscale street food
- Best for: Quality and hygiene
- Specialties: Kachori, samosa, sweets
- Timing: Morning breakfast items

### Chowk (Old City)
- Famous for: Traditional authentic flavors
- Best for: Adventurous food lovers
- Specialties: Chaat items, kebabs
- Timing: Evening street food scene

### Sangam Area
- Famous for: Refreshing items for pilgrims
- Best for: Light snacks and drinks
- Specialties: Lassi, fruit chaat, dahi items
- Timing: All day, especially hot afternoons

## Time-Based Recommendations

### Hot Weather
- Recommend: Dahi-based items, kulfi, lassi, fruit chaat
- Avoid: Heavy fried items during peak heat

### Monsoon Season
- Recommend: Hot items like kachori, samosa, tea
- Popular: Pakoras and hot snacks

### Festival Times (Kumbh, Diwali)
- Special items available
- Increased crowd, longer waits
- Traditional sweets more prominent

## User Preference Mapping

### "Teekha" (Spicy) Requests
- Recommend: Chhole bhature, kebabs, spicy pani puri
- Add: Extra green chutney, green chilies

### "Halka" (Light) Requests  
- Recommend: Dahi bhalla, fruit chaat, lassi
- Avoid: Heavy fried items

### "Meetha" (Sweet) Requests
- Recommend: Jalebi, kulfi, rabri, sweet lassi
- Best timing: After meals or evening

### "Bhookh lagi hai" (Hungry)
- Recommend: Filling items like chhole bhature, bedmi puri
- Suggest: Complete meals rather than snacks

## Cultural Context
- Food is social - sharing is common
- Hygiene varies - mention popular clean spots
- Bargaining is normal at some places
- Religious considerations - many pure veg options
- Seasonal availability affects recommendations
- Local festivals influence food availability
